# IPL Season Chronicles (2008–2025)

This document contains a year-by-year summary of all Indian Premier League seasons, highlighting champions, runner-ups, award winners, and key tournament storylines.

---

## IPL 2008 (Season 1)
- **Winner**: Rajasthan Royals (RR)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: RR won by 3 wickets (chased 164 on the final ball).
- **Orange Cap (Most Runs)**: Shaun Marsh (Kings XI Punjab) - 616 runs
- **Purple Cap (Most Wickets)**: Sohail Tanvir (Rajasthan Royals) - 22 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament (MVP)**: Shane Watson (Rajasthan Royals)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - The inaugural season that launched the global T20 revolution.
  - Brendon McCullum hit 158* in the opening match for KKR against RCB.
  - Shane Warne captained Rajasthan Royals (underdogs) to a fairy-tale championship victory.

## IPL 2009 (Season 2)
- **Winner**: Deccan Chargers (DC)
- **Runner-Up**: Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)
- **Final Result**: Deccan Chargers won by 6 runs (defended 143/6).
- **Orange Cap**: Matthew Hayden (Chennai Super Kings) - 572 runs
- **Purple Cap**: RP Singh (Deccan Chargers) - 23 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Adam Gilchrist (Deccan Chargers)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Hosted in South Africa due to general elections in India.
  - Deccan Chargers, who finished last in 2008, won the tournament under Adam Gilchrist.
  - Anil Kumble took a 5-wicket haul giving away only 5 runs (5/5) for RCB.

## IPL 2010 (Season 3)
- **Winner**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Runner-Up**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Final Result**: CSK won by 22 runs (scored 168/5 and restricted MI to 146/9).
- **Orange Cap**: Sachin Tendulkar (Mumbai Indians) - 618 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Pragyan Ojha (Deccan Chargers) - 21 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Sachin Tendulkar (Mumbai Indians)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - First IPL season to be broadcast live on YouTube.
  - CSK won their maiden title under MS Dhoni.
  - Sachin Tendulkar became the first Indian player to win the Orange Cap and MVP.

## IPL 2011 (Season 4)
- **Winner**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Runner-Up**: Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)
- **Final Result**: CSK won by 58 runs (scored 205/5 and restricted RCB to 147/8).
- **Orange Cap**: Chris Gayle (Royal Challengers Bangalore) - 608 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Lasith Malinga (Mumbai Indians) - 28 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Chris Gayle (Royal Challengers Bangalore)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Number of teams increased to 10 with the addition of Pune Warriors India and Kochi Tuskers Kerala.
  - CSK became the first team to win consecutive IPL titles and win all home matches.
  - Chris Gayle joined RCB mid-season as a replacement and dominated the tournament.

## IPL 2012 (Season 5)
- **Winner**: Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: KKR won by 5 wickets (chased down 191 in Chennai).
- **Orange Cap**: Chris Gayle (Royal Challengers Bangalore) - 733 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Morne Morkel (Delhi Daredevils) - 25 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Sunil Narine (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - KKR won their first title under Gautam Gambhir.
  - Sunil Narine took 24 wickets in his debut season.
  - Chris Gayle hit 59 sixes in the tournament.

## IPL 2013 (Season 6)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 23 runs (scored 148/9 and held CSK to 125/9).
- **Orange Cap**: Michael Hussey (Chennai Super Kings) - 733 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Dwayne Bravo (Chennai Super Kings) - 32 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Shane Watson (Rajasthan Royals)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Rohit Sharma took over MI captaincy mid-season from Ricky Ponting and led them to their first title.
  - Chris Gayle scored his historic 175* against Pune Warriors India.
  - Dwayne Bravo set a record for most wickets in a single IPL season (32 wickets).

## IPL 2014 (Season 7)
- **Winner**: Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Runner-Up**: Kings XI Punjab (KXIP)
- **Final Result**: KKR won by 3 wickets (chased 200, Manish Pandey scored 94).
- **Orange Cap**: Robin Uthappa (Kolkata Knight Riders) - 660 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Mohit Sharma (Chennai Super Kings) - 23 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Glenn Maxwell (Kings XI Punjab)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - The first part of the tournament was played in the UAE due to general elections.
  - KKR won their second title, completing a record 9 consecutive victories.
  - Robin Uthappa won the Orange Cap, scoring ten consecutive 40+ scores.

## IPL 2015 (Season 8)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 41 runs (scored 202/5 and held CSK to 161/8).
- **Orange Cap**: David Warner (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 562 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Dwayne Bravo (Chennai Super Kings) - 26 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Andre Russell (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Mumbai Indians recovered from losing their first 4 matches of the season to win the championship.
  - AB de Villiers and Virat Kohli scored a record partnership of 215* against MI.

## IPL 2016 (Season 9)
- **Winner**: Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH)
- **Runner-Up**: Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)
- **Final Result**: SRH won by 8 runs (scored 208/7 and restricted RCB to 200/7).
- **Orange Cap**: Virat Kohli (Royal Challengers Bangalore) - 973 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Bhuvneshwar Kumar (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 23 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Virat Kohli (Royal Challengers Bangalore)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - CSK and RR were suspended; two new temporary franchises competed: Gujarat Lions and Rising Pune Supergiants.
  - Virat Kohli set the all-time batting record, scoring 973 runs with 4 centuries.
  - Sunrisers Hyderabad won their first championship under David Warner.

## IPL 2017 (Season 10)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Rising Pune Supergiant (RPS)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 1 run (defended 129, restricting RPS to 128/6).
- **Orange Cap**: David Warner (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 641 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Bhuvneshwar Kumar (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 26 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Sunil Narine (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Mumbai Indians won their third title in a thrilling 1-run finish against Pune.
  - Mitchell Johnson defended 11 runs in the final over of the match.
  - Bhuvneshwar Kumar became the first bowler to retain the Purple Cap.

## IPL 2018 (Season 11)
- **Winner**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Runner-Up**: Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH)
- **Final Result**: CSK won by 8 wickets (chased 179, Shane Watson scored 117*).
- **Orange Cap**: Kane Williamson (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 735 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Andrew Tye (Kings XI Punjab) - 24 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Sunil Narine (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - CSK and RR returned from suspension.
  - CSK won the championship under MS Dhoni with a squad dubbed "Dad's Army" due to many players being over 30 years old.
  - Rashid Khan put in outstanding all-round performances for SRH.

## IPL 2019 (Season 12)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 1 run (scored 149/8, CSK finished on 148/7).
- **Orange Cap**: David Warner (Sunrisers Hyderabad) - 692 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Imran Tahir (Chennai Super Kings) - 26 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Andre Russell (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Mumbai Indians won their fourth title in another 1-run final against CSK.
  - Lasith Malinga bowled a slow yorker to dismiss Shardul Thakur on the last ball of the match.
  - Andre Russell hit 52 sixes and won his second MVP award.

## IPL 2020 (Season 13)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Delhi Capitals (DC)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 5 wickets (chased down 157 in 18.4 overs).
- **Orange Cap**: KL Rahul (Kings XI Punjab) - 670 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Kagiso Rabada (Delhi Capitals) - 30 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Jofra Archer (Rajasthan Royals)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Played entirely in the UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah) behind closed doors due to COVID-19.
  - Mumbai Indians dominated the season to secure their fifth title.
  - Delhi Capitals reached their first-ever final.

## IPL 2021 (Season 14)
- **Winner**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Runner-Up**: Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Final Result**: CSK won by 27 runs (scored 192/3, KKR made 165/9).
- **Orange Cap**: Ruturaj Gaikwad (Chennai Super Kings) - 635 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Harshal Patel (Royal Challengers Bangalore) - 32 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Harshal Patel (Royal Challengers Bangalore)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Suspended in India in May due to COVID-19 cases, resumed in the UAE in September.
  - CSK won their fourth title under MS Dhoni.
  - Harshal Patel tied Dwayne Bravo's record of 32 wickets in a single season.

## IPL 2022 (Season 15)
- **Winner**: Gujarat Titans (GT)
- **Runner-Up**: Rajasthan Royals (RR)
- **Final Result**: GT won by 7 wickets (chased 130 in 18.1 overs).
- **Orange Cap**: Jos Buttler (Rajasthan Royals) - 863 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Yuzvendra Chahal (Rajasthan Royals) - 27 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Jos Buttler (Rajasthan Royals)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Introduction of two new teams: Gujarat Titans and Lucknow Super Giants, expanding the league back to 10 teams.
  - Hardik Pandya led debutants Gujarat Titans to the title in front of 100,000+ fans at Narendra Modi Stadium.
  - Jos Buttler hit 4 centuries in a single season.

## IPL 2023 (Season 16)
- **Winner**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Runner-Up**: Gujarat Titans (GT)
- **Final Result**: CSK won by 5 wickets (DLS method, chased 171 in 15 overs on a rain-delayed reserve day).
- **Orange Cap**: Shubman Gill (Gujarat Titans) - 890 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Mohammed Shami (Gujarat Titans) - 28 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Shubman Gill (Gujarat Titans)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - CSK won their fifth title in a dramatic finish. Ravindra Jadeja hit a six and a four off Mohit Sharma's final two balls to win the match.
  - Introduction of the "Impact Player" substitution rule.
  - Shubman Gill scored 3 centuries and 890 runs.

## IPL 2024 (Season 17)
- **Winner**: Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Runner-Up**: Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH)
- **Final Result**: KKR won by 8 wickets (chased down 113 in 10.3 overs).
- **Orange Cap**: Virat Kohli (Royal Challengers Bangalore) - 741 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Harshal Patel (Punjab Kings) - 24 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Sunil Narine (Kolkata Knight Riders)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - KKR dominated the league under mentor Gautam Gambhir, winning their third title.
  - SRH registered record-breaking team totals of 277 and 287.
  - Sunil Narine won his third MVP award for scoring 488 runs and taking 17 wickets.
  - RCB won 6 consecutive matches to complete a miracle playoff qualification.

## IPL 2025 (Season 18)
- **Winner**: Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Runner-Up**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Final Result**: MI won by 6 wickets (chased 182, Suryakumar Yadav scored 78*).
- **Orange Cap**: Yashasvi Jaiswal (Rajasthan Royals) - 765 runs
- **Purple Cap**: Jasprit Bumrah (Mumbai Indians) - 26 wickets
- **Player of the Tournament**: Suryakumar Yadav (Mumbai Indians)
- **Key Highlights**:
  - Followed a highly anticipated Mega Auction.
  - MI reclaimed the title under the captaincy of Suryakumar Yadav.
  - Jasprit Bumrah dominated with an economy rate of 6.2 across the tournament to secure the Purple Cap.
