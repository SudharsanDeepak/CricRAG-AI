# IPL Tournament Rules & Match Regulations

This document outlines the rules, game regulations, and structural guidelines of the Indian Premier League (IPL).

---

## 1. Match Format & Duration
- **Overs**: Each match consists of two innings. Each innings is limited to a maximum of 20 overs (120 legal balls) unless shortened due to weather conditions.
- **Players**: Each team fields 11 players. A maximum of 4 overseas (foreign) players can be included in the playing XI at any time.

## 2. Powerplay Rules
- **Duration**: The first 6 overs of each innings (Overs 1.1 to 6.0) constitute the batting Powerplay.
- **Fielding Restrictions**:
  - During the Powerplay, a maximum of **two fielding players** are allowed outside the 30-yard inner circle.
  - After the Powerplay (from over 7 onwards), a maximum of **five fielding players** are allowed outside the 30-yard circle.
- **Purpose**: Powerplay rules encourage aggressive batting at the beginning of the innings by reducing boundary protection.

## 3. Strategic Timeouts
- **Frequency**: There are **four Strategic Timeouts** in a standard IPL match (two per innings).
- **Duration**: Each timeout lasts exactly **2 minutes and 30 seconds**.
- **Timing**:
  - **Bowling Team's Timeout**: Can be taken between overs 6 and 9 (at the end of over 6, 7, 8, or 9).
  - **Batting Team's Timeout**: Can be taken between overs 11 and 16 (at the end of over 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, or 16).
- **Purpose**: Allows teams to plan tactics and gives broadcasters a commercial slot.

## 4. Impact Player Rule
- **Introduction**: Introduced in the IPL 2023 season.
- **Concept**:
  - Teams must name **5 substitute players** at the toss alongside their playing XI.
  - One of these substitutes can be introduced as an "Impact Player" at any stage during a team's batting or bowling innings.
- **Conditions**:
  - The substitute can replace any player in the active XI.
  - If the active XI already contains 4 overseas players, the Impact Player introduced must be an **Indian player** (an overseas player can only be an Impact Player if the active XI contains fewer than 4 overseas players).
  - The replaced player cannot take any further part in the match.
  - The Impact Player can bowl a full quota of 4 overs and bat at any position, regardless of what the replaced player had already done.

## 5. Super Over (Tie-Breaker)
- **Applicability**: Used when a match ends in a tie (both teams score the exact same number of runs at the end of their 20 overs, regardless of wickets lost).
- **Rules**:
  - Each team bats for one over (6 balls) unless they lose 2 wickets, which ends their Super Over innings.
  - The team that batted second in the main match bats first in the Super Over.
  - The team that scores the most runs in the Super Over wins the match.
  - **Multiple Super Overs**: If the Super Over itself is tied, another Super Over is played. This process repeats until a clear winner emerges. (This rule was updated after the famous tied Super Over in the 2019 ICC World Cup final. Previously, boundary counts were used, but now consecutive Super Overs are played).

## 6. Net Run Rate (NRR) Calculation
- **Purpose**: Net Run Rate is used to rank teams on the points table if they are tied on points.
- **Formula**:
  $$\text{Net Run Rate (NRR)} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Runs Scored by Team}}{\text{Total Overs Faced by Team}} \right) - \left( \frac{\text{Total Runs Conceded against Team}}{\text{Total Overs Bowled against Team}} \right)$$
- **Special Conditions**:
  - If a team is bowled out before completing their 20 overs, the calculation uses the full **20 overs** as the overs faced.
  - Only matches with completed results (not abandoned or declared "No Result") are counted towards the NRR.

## 7. Tournament Points System
- **Win**: 2 points
- **Loss**: 0 points
- **No Result / Abandoned Match**: 1 point to each team

## 8. Orange & Purple Caps
- **Orange Cap**: Awarded to the player who scores the **most runs** in the tournament. During the tournament, the current leading run-scorer wears the physical Orange Cap on the field. The overall leader at the end of the final match wins the award.
- **Purple Cap**: Awarded to the bowler who takes the **most wickets** in the tournament. During the tournament, the leading wicket-taker wears the physical Purple Cap. If there is a tie on wickets, the bowler with the better economy rate is ranked higher.

## 9. DRS (Decision Review System)
- **Quota**: Each team is allowed **two unsuccessful DRS reviews** per innings.
- **Scope**: Reviews can be used to challenge decisions on:
  - Outs (LBW, catches, run-outs, stumpings).
  - **Wides & No-Balls**: Starting in IPL 2023, players can review on-field decisions regarding wides and waist-high no-balls.

## 10. Free Hit Rule
- **Trigger**: Called immediately after any bowler bowls a **No-Ball** (front foot, height, fielding restriction violation, etc.).
- **Rule**:
  - The next delivery is designated as a "Free Hit".
  - The batsman cannot be dismissed on a Free Hit delivery except under specific conditions: Run Out, Obstructing the Field, or Hit the Ball Twice.
  - If the Free Hit delivery is a wide or a no-ball, the next delivery becomes a Free Hit.

## 11. Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) Method
- **Trigger**: Used during rain or external interruptions when play cannot be completed in full.
- **Calculation**: Calculates revised targets based on the resources remaining (overs left to bat and wickets lost) for both teams at the time of the stoppage.
- **Minimum overs**: A minimum of **5 overs** must be bowled in the second innings to constitute a completed match and apply the DLS method.

## 12. Auction & Retention Rules
- **Mega Auction**: Happens once every few seasons (typically 3-4 years). Teams must release most players, keeping only a small number of retained players.
- **Mini Auction**: Happens in years without a Mega Auction. Teams can retain the majority of their squad and auction for released or new players.
- **Retention Limits**: In Mega Auctions, teams are typically allowed to retain up to **4 to 6 players** (either through direct retention or Right to Match cards) under strict budget salary caps.
- **Salary Cap**: Each team is allocated a maximum purse (budget) to spend on building their squad (e.g., 120 Crore INR).
