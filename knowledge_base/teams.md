# IPL Teams Directory & Franchise Histories

This document details the histories, home grounds, key player legacies, championships, and statistics for the 10 Indian Premier League franchises.

## 1. Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
- **Home Ground**: M. A. Chidambaram Stadium (Chepauk), Chennai
- **Team Colors**: Yellow
- **Trophies Won**: 5 (2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, 2023)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 5 (2008, 2012, 2013, 2015, 2019)
- **All-Time Key Players**: MS Dhoni, Suresh Raina, Ravindra Jadeja, Dwayne Bravo, Faf du Plessis, Michael Hussey, Ruturaj Gaikwad.
- **Rivalry**: Chennai Super Kings vs Mumbai Indians is widely considered the "El Clásico" of the IPL. The two teams have faced each other in 4 finals (2010, 2013, 2015, 2019), with MI winning three of those finals.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Known as the most consistent team in IPL history, qualifying for the playoffs in 12 out of the 14 seasons they participated in.
  - Suspended for two seasons (2016 and 2017) due to spot-fixing investigations, but made a historic comeback in 2018 under Dhoni to win the title (often referred to as the "Dad's Army" victory due to the high average age of the players).
  - Dwayne Bravo won the Purple Cap twice for CSK (2013 with a record 32 wickets, and 2015).
  - Ruturaj Gaikwad took over the captaincy of CSK from MS Dhoni ahead of the 2024 season.

## 2. Mumbai Indians (MI)
- **Home Ground**: Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai
- **Team Colors**: Blue and Gold
- **Trophies Won**: 5 (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2010)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Rohit Sharma, Sachin Tendulkar, Lasith Malinga, Jasprit Bumrah, Kieron Pollard, Hardik Pandya, Krunal Pandya, Suryakumar Yadav.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - First team to win 5 IPL trophies.
  - Lasith Malinga was their bowling spearhead, taking 170 wickets for the franchise and bowling the famous final delivery in the 2019 final to secure a 1-run win against CSK.
  - MI won the Champions League Twenty20 twice (2011, 2013).
  - Under Rohit Sharma's captaincy from 2013 to 2023, the team built a dominant dynasty. Hardik Pandya was named captain for the 2024 season, sparking significant fan reactions.
  - Wankhede Stadium is known for being batting-friendly with short boundaries, leading to high-scoring encounters.

## 3. Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
- **Home Ground**: Eden Gardens, Kolkata
- **Team Colors**: Purple and Gold
- **Trophies Won**: 3 (2012, 2014, 2024)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2021)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Gautam Gambhir, Sunil Narine, Andre Russell, Yusuf Pathan, Robin Uthappa, Shakib Al Hasan, Shreyas Iyer.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Won their first two titles in 2012 and 2014 under the captaincy of Gautam Gambhir.
  - Gautam Gambhir returned to the franchise as a mentor in 2024, leading KKR under Shreyas Iyer's captaincy to a dominant third title, defeating SRH in the final.
  - KKR holds the record for the longest winning streak by an IPL team: 10 consecutive wins (9 in IPL 2014 and 1 in IPL 2015).
  - Sunil Narine and Andre Russell are the core legends of KKR, winning multiple MVP awards.

## 4. Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)
- **Home Ground**: M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bengaluru
- **Team Colors**: Red, Black, and Gold
- **Trophies Won**: 0
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 3 (2009, 2011, 2016)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Virat Kohli, AB de Villiers, Chris Gayle, Yuzvendra Chahal, Glenn Maxwell, Faf du Plessis, Anil Kumble.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Known for having one of the most passionate and loyal fan bases in the IPL, using the slogan "Ee Sala Cup Namde" (This year the cup is ours).
  - Chinnaswamy Stadium is notorious for high altitude and extremely short boundaries, making it a paradise for batsmen.
  - RCB holds the record for the highest team total in IPL history: 263/5 against Pune Warriors India in 2013 (led by Chris Gayle's 175*).
  - RCB also holds the record for the lowest team total in IPL history: 49 all out against Kolkata Knight Riders at Eden Gardens in 2017.
  - Made a spectacular run of 6 consecutive wins in the second half of IPL 2024 to qualify for the playoffs from the bottom of the table.

## 5. Rajasthan Royals (RR)
- **Home Ground**: Sawai Mansingh Stadium, Jaipur
- **Team Colors**: Pink and Royal Blue
- **Trophies Won**: 1 (2008)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2022)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Shane Warne, Jos Buttler, Sanju Samson, Yuzvendra Chahal, Shane Watson, Ajinkya Rahane, Ravichandran Ashwin.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Won the inaugural edition of the IPL in 2008 under the inspirational captaincy and coaching of legendary Australian leg-spinner Shane Warne, as underdogs (referred to as "Shane's Shane-anigans" or the "unheralded champions").
  - Suspended for two seasons (2016 and 2017) alongside CSK.
  - Jos Buttler scored 4 centuries in the 2022 season, tying Virat Kohli's record for second most in a single season.
  - Sanju Samson leads the team as captain, known for his elegant batting style.

## 6. Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH) / Deccan Chargers
- **Home Ground**: Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium, Hyderabad
- **Team Colors**: Orange and Black
- **Trophies Won**: 2 (2009 as Deccan Chargers; 2016 as Sunrisers Hyderabad)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 2 (2018, 2024)
- **All-Time Key Players**: David Warner, Bhuvneshwar Kumar, Rashid Khan, Kane Williamson, Pat Cummins, Abhishek Sharma, Travis Head.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Deccan Chargers won the 2009 IPL held in South Africa under Adam Gilchrist. The franchise was terminated in 2012, and Sunrisers Hyderabad was created.
  - SRH won the 2016 IPL under David Warner's captaincy, defeating RCB in a high-scoring final.
  - Bhuvneshwar Kumar won the Purple Cap in consecutive seasons (2016 and 2017) for SRH.
  - In IPL 2024, captained by Pat Cummins, SRH broke the record for the highest team score in IPL history twice, scoring 277/3 against MI and then 287/3 against RCB. Their opening pair of Travis Head and Abhishek Sharma revolutionized powerplay batting.

## 7. Delhi Capitals (DC) / Delhi Daredevils
- **Home Ground**: Arun Jaitley Stadium, New Delhi
- **Team Colors**: Blue and Red
- **Trophies Won**: 0
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2020)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Rishabh Pant, Virender Sehwag, Shreyas Iyer, Shikhar Dhawan, Amit Mishra, Kagiso Rabada, David Warner.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Rebranded from Delhi Daredevils to Delhi Capitals in December 2018.
  - Reached their first and only final in 2020 under the captaincy of Shreyas Iyer and coaching of Ricky Ponting, losing to Mumbai Indians.
  - Rishabh Pant has been the franchise's marquee player, returning to captain them in 2024 after recovering from a life-threatening car accident.
  - Amit Mishra took three IPL hat-tricks, two of them while playing for Delhi.

## 8. Punjab Kings (PBKS) / Kings XI Punjab
- **Home Ground**: PCA Stadium, Mohali / Maharaja Yadavindra Singh Cricket Stadium, Mullanpur
- **Team Colors**: Red and Silver
- **Trophies Won**: 0
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2014)
- **All-Time Key Players**: KL Rahul, Shaun Marsh, Glenn Maxwell, David Miller, Ravichandran Ashwin, Shikhar Dhawan, Arshdeep Singh.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Rebranded from Kings XI Punjab to Punjab Kings in 2021.
  - Reached the finals in 2014 under George Bailey, where they lost to Kolkata Knight Riders despite a century from Wriddhiman Saha.
  - Shaun Marsh won the Orange Cap in the inaugural 2008 season, scoring 616 runs as an uncapped player.
  - Known for frequent changes in their coaching staff and captaincy.

## 9. Gujarat Titans (GT)
- **Home Ground**: Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad
- **Team Colors**: Navy Blue and Gold
- **Trophies Won**: 1 (2022)
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 1 (2023)
- **All-Time Key Players**: Hardik Pandya, Rashid Khan, Shubman Gill, Mohammed Shami, David Miller.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Joined the IPL in 2022 as one of two new expansion franchises.
  - Won the championship in their debut season (2022) under Hardik Pandya's captaincy, defeating Rajasthan Royals at their massive home ground in Ahmedabad.
  - Reached the final in 2023, losing a rain-shortened thriller to CSK.
  - Shubman Gill won the Orange Cap in 2023 (890 runs, including 3 centuries) and was appointed captain for the 2024 season.

## 10. Lucknow Super Giants (LSG)
- **Home Ground**: BRSABV Ekana Cricket Stadium, Lucknow
- **Team Colors**: Dark Blue
- **Trophies Won**: 0
- **Runners-Up Finishes**: 0 (Playoffs in 2022, 2023)
- **All-Time Key Players**: KL Rahul, Quinton de Kock, Marcus Stoinis, Nicholas Pooran, Ravi Bishnoi, Mayank Yadav.
- **Notable Facts**:
  - Joined the IPL in 2022 as the second new expansion franchise, bought for a record price.
  - Qualified for the playoffs in both 2022 and 2023 under the captaincy of KL Rahul and mentorship of Gautam Gambhir.
  - Ekana Stadium in Lucknow is known for its slow, spin-friendly pitches.
  - Fast bowler Mayank Yadav made headlines in 2024 by bowling at speeds of 156.7 km/h in his debut matches.
